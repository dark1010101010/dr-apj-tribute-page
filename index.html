TIMELINE

OCTOBER 15, 1931

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, into a humble family. His father was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque. Despite financial struggles, Kalam developed a strong interest in science and learning from a young age.

Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli. This marked his first academic step toward a career in science.

1954

1955

Enrolled in Aerospace Engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT). Here, he developed a passion for aircraft design and space technology.

Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist. He began working on designing small hovercraft prototypes.

1960

1969

Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He became the Project Director of India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).

Successfully launched Rohini satellite into orbit using SLV-III, making India a member of the exclusive space club.

1980

1981

Received the Padma Bhushan, India’s third-highest civilian award, for his contribution to science and engineering.

Led India’s Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP), overseeing development of missiles like Agni and Prithvi.

1983

1990

Awarded the Padma Vibhushan, India’s second-highest civilian award, for exceptional work in defense and space technology.

Appointed Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of DRDO. Played a key role in strengthening India's defense systems.

1992

1997

Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian honor, recognizing his immense contribution to science and national development.

Played a crucial role in India’s Pokhran-II nuclear tests, making India a nuclear-capable nation.

1998

1999

Became Principal Scientific Adviser to the Government of India, shaping national science and technology policies.

Elected as the 11th President of India. He became widely known as the “People’s President” for his humility and connection with students.

2002

2007

Completed his presidential term and returned to teaching, inspiring millions of students across India.

Launched the “What Can I Give Movement,” encouraging youth to contribute to nation-building and eliminate corruption.

2011

JULY 27, 2015

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam collapsed while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong and passed away at the age of 83. He died doing what he loved most—teaching and inspiring students.

LEGACY

Dr. Kalam remains one of India’s most beloved leaders, remembered for his humility, vision, and dedication to education, science, and youth empowerment. His life continues to inspire millions to dream big and serve the nation.
